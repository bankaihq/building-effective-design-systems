### Semantics, ### or the philosophy of meaning Semantics talks about how things do not exist on their own, but always in a context. Notes: This context is like a domain, and within a domain you always have a shared language. Like a nation's culture, or a certain industry or a field of science. Within each of those domains new concepts are born each day, and each time those concepts need a name.
### Shared language Names allow us to discuss a subject without it being present. Notes: Names allow us to discuss a subject without it being present. Like if you want to talk about John from accounting an how he is is really into bonsai. Try doing this without the use of names AND examples! This is also true if you are building systems. Those systems will inherently contain new components, th need meaningful names. So they can be used effectively in communication. It is good to give it a name that makes sense to everyone on the team and maybe even in the organisation. This is what shared language is.

# Practical Tips - Reason from another's perspective, not your own. - Avoid colloquialisms (Typically local ways of saying things) - Use simple words - Stay away from abbreviations. - Make a name longer vs shorter. Notes: - Reason from another's perspective, not your own. - Avoid colloquialisms (Typically local ways of saying things). For example, native English speaker's vocabulary is naturally al lot bigger than non-native ones. So they might know that one specific term for something, but those might very well be completely unknown to others. - Use simple words, - Stay away from abbreviations. They make perfect sense to you, but need to be learned. Especially abbreviations that are not part of common language like 'idx', and 'EOB'. - Make a name longer vs shorter. Longer names, although verbose, tend to be more descriptive.
## Naming things is a creative effort, so find some inspiration. - In a **thesaurus** (like [thesaurus.com/](https://www.thesaurus.com/)) - In the field of **semantics** (like [schema.org](http://schema.org)) - Ask a co-worker or a friend. And brainstorm a little. Two minds are more than one.
## Iterate on a name. Notes: (nice little diagram on why iteration in creative process is useful) Sometimes it can be really hard to predefine the functionality or purpose of an item. To make your life easier start with a 'working title' and update it throughout the development process.
